### Available Biological Processes (9 experiments)

#### 1. **Photosynthesis üå±**
- **Duration**: 10 seconds
- **Description**: Plants convert light energy into chemical energy (glucose)
- **Equation**: 6CO‚ÇÇ + 6H‚ÇÇO + Light ‚Üí C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇO‚ÇÜ + 6O‚ÇÇ
- **Key Observations**:
  - Oxygen bubbles released
  - Glucose accumulates
  - Chlorophyll fluorescence
  - CO‚ÇÇ decreases
- **Equipment**: Light source, plant leaves, microscope
- **Data Collected**: O‚ÇÇ Production, CO‚ÇÇ Consumption, Light Intensity, Glucose

#### 2. **Aerobic Respiration ‚ö°**
- **Duration**: 8 seconds
- **Description**: Cells use oxygen to generate ATP energy
- **Equation**: C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇO‚ÇÜ + 6O‚ÇÇ ‚Üí 6CO‚ÇÇ + 6H‚ÇÇO + Energy (ATP)
- **Key Observations**:
  - O‚ÇÇ decreases rapidly
  - Temperature increases (2-5¬∞C)
  - CO‚ÇÇ produced
  - Energy generation
- **Equipment**: Respirometer, thermometer, oxygen sensor
- **Data Collected**: O‚ÇÇ Consumption, Temperature, CO‚ÇÇ Production, ATP

#### 3. **Anaerobic Fermentation üß´**
- **Duration**: 12 seconds
- **Description**: Microorganisms break down glucose without oxygen
- **Equation**: C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇO‚ÇÜ ‚Üí 2C‚ÇÇH‚ÇÖOH + 2CO‚ÇÇ
- **Key Observations**:
  - Vigorous bubbling (CO‚ÇÇ)
  - Alcohol smell
  - pH decreases
  - Foam rises
- **Equipment**: Fermentation vessel, pH meter, gas collection
- **Data Collected**: CO‚ÇÇ Production, Ethanol %, pH, Temperature

#### 4. **Enzyme Catalysis üß¨**
- **Duration**: 6 seconds
- **Description**: Enzymes speed up biochemical reactions
- **Reaction**: Starch + Amylase ‚Üí Maltose + Glucose
- **Key Observations**:
  - Reaction rate increases
  - Blue-black color fades
  - Temperature sensitive
  - Enzyme not consumed
- **Equipment**: Spectrophotometer, water bath, enzyme solution
- **Data Collected**: Reaction Rate, Temperature, Enzyme Concentration, Substrate %
- **Temperature Effects**: Optimal at ~37¬∞C (310K)

#### 5. **Mitosis (Cell Division) üî¨**
- **Duration**: 15 seconds
- **Description**: Nuclear division creating two identical daughter cells
- **Phases**:
  - Prophase: Chromatin condenses
  - Metaphase: Chromosomes align
  - Anaphase: Chromosomes separate
  - Telophase: Nuclear envelope reforms
  - Cytokinesis: Two daughter cells
- **Equipment**: Microscope, staining kit, onion root
- **Data Collected**: Cell Cycle Duration, Mitotic Index, Phase Duration, Cell Count

#### 6. **DNA Extraction üß¨**
- **Duration**: 5 seconds
- **Description**: Isolate and visualize DNA using chemical precipitation
- **Materials**: Onion/strawberry, soap, salt, ethanol
- **Procedure**:
  1. Chop tissue
  2. Add soap (breaks membranes)
  3. Add salt
  4. Filter
  5. Add cold ethanol
  6. DNA precipitates
- **Key Observations**:
  - White DNA strands appear
  - Stringy and fibrous
  - Visible to naked eye
- **Data Collected**: DNA Yield, Purity, Concentration, Fragment Size

#### 7. **Bacterial Growth Kinetics ü¶†**
- **Duration**: 20 seconds
- **Description**: Monitor bacterial population through growth phases
- **Growth Phases**:
  - Lag Phase: Initial slow growth
  - Log Phase: Exponential growth
  - Stationary Phase: Growth plateau
  - Death Phase: Decline
- **Equipment**: Culture plates, incubator, spectrophotometer
- **Data Collected**: Cell Density (OD600), CFU/mL, Generation Time, Growth Rate

#### 8. **Osmosis & Diffusion üíß**
- **Duration**: 10 seconds
- **Description**: Movement through semipermeable membranes
- **Types**:
  - Diffusion: Solute moves from high to low concentration
  - Osmosis: Water moves toward high solute concentration
- **Materials**: Dialysis bags, food coloring, sucrose, salt
- **Key Observations**:
  - Bag becomes turgid
  - Color spreads
  - Water enters
  - Equilibrium reached
- **Data Collected**: Mass Change, Water Uptake, Time to Equilibrium, Concentration

#### 9. **Protein Synthesis üß¨**
- **Duration**: 8 seconds
- **Description**: Simulate translation (mRNA ‚Üí tRNA ‚Üí Protein)
- **Process**:
  1. mRNA on ribosome
  2. tRNA brings amino acids
  3. Peptide bonds form
  4. Ribosome moves
  5. Protein folds
- **Equipment**: Molecular models, color-coded components
- **Key Observations**:
  - Protein chain grows
  - Correct sequence critical
  - Codon reading sequential
- **Data Collected**: Translation Rate, Codon Count, Protein Length, Error Rate
